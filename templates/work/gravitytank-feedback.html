End-to-End Prototyping

gravitytank Internal Reviews Redefined

From May to August 2014, I worked with gravitytank to reimagine their internal staff review experience. They presented me with a problem and a concept. 10 weeks later I gave them a user-centric, functional, and high-fidelity prototype of a feedback system that they continue to evolve today.

gravitytank's previous review system was a traditional one—Staff performs work and their superiors review their performance based on predefined institutional metrics. This wasn’t working for anyone for a number of reasons:

  1. The review was one-directional
  2. The process broke the staff’s naturally ocurring feedback flow
  3. The results of the process produced little-to-no value
  4. The review tool was archaic in design and technology

My Role

Because innovation is gravitytank’s bread and butter, they discovered a potential solution to their internal review problem. As users of the product they found themselves too close to the project, and needed an objective outsider to help validate their concept and ease them into a self-design process. I was the objective outsider—better known as the interaction design contractor.

  • Scope Definition

    I partnered with gravitytank operations and interaction design leadership, human resources, and engineering to define the core value and scope of the feedback prototype. The feedback application enhances the naturally ocurring feedback between gravitytank staff.

  • Product Ownership

    From concept to prototype I shaped the feedback application by managing the design and development schedule, performing the design and development work, negotiating and prioritizing features, leading weekly team check-ins, and adjusting accordingly.

Lean, Agile, & Human-Centered

In order to deliver a complete product prototype in 10 weeks I applied lean UX principles, epicenter design, rapid prototyping methods, and weekly development sprints.

  • Get Smart

    3 weeks

    I performed formative research to learn about gravitytank staff, structure, and business; created a draft schedule; mapped my approach; mapped the system; and generated consensus with stakeholders.

    Toolkit

    HR documents; user interviews; Basecamp; user stories; sketches: user flows, scope diagrams, entity relationship models, and CRUD views; and closed card sorting.

  • Sketch to Code

    6 weeks

    I used notes and sketches generated in the Get Smart stage to produce a key application feature for the team to test, review, and discuss.

    Toolkit

    SCRUM board, Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Devise, Bower, Foundation, HTML, CSS, AngularJS, GitHub, Heroku, and Google Sheets for bug tracking.

  • Deliver and Reflect

    1 week

    During the last week of the project I met regularly with the production team for general walk-through, to discuss pending product decisions, and to fix as many technical bugs as possible. My final check-in was with senior leadership in operations and interaction design. I asked them for brutally honest feedback and they gave it.
