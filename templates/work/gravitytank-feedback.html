End-to-End Prototyping

gravitytank Internal Reviews Redefined

From May to August 2014, I worked with gravitytank to reimagine their internal staff review experience. They presented me with a problem and a concept; 10 weeks later I gave them a user-centric and high-fidelity web application prototype called Feedback, which they continue to evolve today.

gravitytank's review system was a traditional one, and not providing the value expected by those involved in the review process. Innovation is gravitytankâ€™s bread and butter, so they came up with a great idea on how to get the value they were looking for.

Their idea was to do away with traditional performance reviews, and create a mechanism that empowers staff to declare their professional goals and receive the contextual feedback to help achieve them. The solution was creating the Feedback web application.

My Role

My role as interaction designer on this project involved three main responsibilities:

  1. Objectivity
  2. Product Ownership
  3. High-fidelity Prototyping

  • Objectivity

    gravitytank has the capabilities to design and engineer web applications. As users of the Feedback application, they felt that it would be a missed opportunity to not include an objective party in the early stages of product design. It was my role to think outside of gravitytank, and challenge their idea by finding and using multiple perspectives—including my own.

  • Product Ownership

    I partnered with key stakeholders at gravitytank for guidance, but during my time on the project I owned Feedback. It was my responsibility to help define the core value of the application and interpret it's vision. We defined the core value as enhancing the naturally occurring feedback between gravitytank staff.

  • Full-Stack Prototyping

    I shaped the feedback application by managing the design and development schedule, performing the design and development work, negotiating and prioritizing features, leading weekly team check-ins, and adjusting accordingly.

Lean, Agile, & Human-Centered

In order to deliver a complete product prototype in 10 weeks I applied lean UX principles, epicenter design, rapid prototyping methods, and weekly development sprints.

  • Get Smart

    3 weeks

    I performed formative research to learn about gravitytank staff, structure, and business; created a draft schedule; mapped my approach; mapped the system; and generated consensus with stakeholders.

    Toolkit

    HR documents; user interviews; Basecamp; user stories; sketches: user flows, scope diagrams, entity relationship models, and CRUD views; and closed card sorting.

  • Sketch to Code

    6 weeks

    I used notes and sketches generated in the Get Smart stage to produce a key application feature for the team to test, review, and discuss.

    Toolkit

    SCRUM board, Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Devise, Bower, Foundation, HTML, CSS, AngularJS, GitHub, Heroku, and Google Sheets for bug tracking.

  • Deliver and Reflect

    1 week

    During the last week of the project I met regularly with the production team for general walk-through, to discuss pending product decisions, and to fix as many technical bugs as possible. My final check-in was with senior leadership in operations and interaction design. I asked them for brutally honest feedback and they gave it.
